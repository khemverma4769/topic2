Activity 1

In the context of Internet of Things (IoT), smart lighting systems typically require certain information to be stored and maintained for each light. This information can be divided into two categories: state information and configuration information. State information includes the current on/off status, dimming level, color temperature, and color of the light. This information is needed to control the light and to display its current state in the user interface of the smart lighting system. Configuration information includes the unique identifier of the light, its physical location, type (e.g. dimmable, color-changing), and any other relevant information such as supported features, firmware version, and network information.

Activity 2

Smart air conditioning refers to the integration of home automation technology into air conditioning systems to allow for remote control and monitoring. To implement smart air conditioning, the air conditioning system needs to be connected to the Internet and a smart thermostat or home automation hub is typically required. The specific requirements will vary based on the make and model of the air conditioning system and the type of home automation technology being used.

Activity 3

In a large commercial building, smart lighting and air conditioning systems can interact to create a more efficient and comfortable environment. This interaction can be achieved through building automation technology and the IoT. Other connected devices, such as occupancy sensors and smart window shades, can also be used to create a more efficient and comfortable environment.
